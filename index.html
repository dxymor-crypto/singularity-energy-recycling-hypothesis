Singularity Energy Recycling Hypothesis

Proposed by COLA — 2025.

The Singularity Energy Recycling Hypothesis suggests that black holes are not merely gravitational sinks, but rather act as cosmic energy recovery mechanisms within the broader universal cycle. According to this model, when matter and energy are absorbed by black holes, they are not destroyed — instead, they transition into fundamental states beyond the observable boundary of spacetime.

These energy states may accumulate “beyond the singularity” — in a non-observable domain or thin membrane of energy continuity. Over vast cosmological timescales, this stored energy could contribute to the re-initiation of universal expansion — suggesting that black holes may prepare the foundation for the next cosmological cycle, a re-birth from a singularity event.

This concept connects black hole dynamics, singularity theory, and cyclic cosmology in a speculative but unified framework of energy transformation — viewing the universe not as a one-time explosion, but as an iterative energy system that continuously collapses and reforms itself.
